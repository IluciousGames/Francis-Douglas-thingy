Vatican City

A city-state and the smallest country in the world...

  • Population: 764 (as of the latest data).
  • Size: 0.44 sq km.
  • Theocracy: A state governed by divine guidance.
  • Famous resident: The Pope.
  • Founded: February 11, 1929, with the Lateran Treaty.

Interesting Facts

  • No prison—offenders are sent to Italian jails.
  • It has the only ATM in the world with instructions in Latin.
  • It has one of the highest crime rates per capita due to pickpocketing.
  • It has its own postal service, faster than Italy’s.

History of Christianity

Christianity began in the 1st century AD...

  • Founder: Jesus Christ.
  • Beliefs: Jesus is the Son of God and Messiah.
  • Key Event: Legalization of Christianity in 313 AD by Emperor Constantine.

Buenos Aires

Pope Francis was born in this city...

  • Country: Argentina.
  • Founded: 1536, re-established in 1580.
  • Nickname: Porteño (Person of the port).

Interesting Facts

  • Widest avenue in the world—Avenida 9 de Julio.
  • Most bookstores per capita in the world.
  • Known for tango and its European influence.

St. Francis of Assisi

  • Full Name: Giovanni di Pietro di Bernardone.
  • Born: 1181 or 1182, Italy.
  • Death: October 3, 1226.
  • Notable for: Founded the Franciscan Order.

Interesting Facts

  • Renounced wealth for a life of poverty.
  • Believed to have received the stigmata.
  • Created the first nativity scene in 1223.
